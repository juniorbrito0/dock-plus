Get started

Up and running
in two minutes

Install Dock+, arrange your widgets, and grant a few macOS permissions. Here's the whole thing, plus exactly why each permission is asked for.

Install

  1. Download & move to Applications

    Download Dock+, then drag Dock+.app into your /Applications folder.

  2. Open it

    Double-click to launch. On first run, right-click → Open if macOS asks. Dock+ is a menu-bar app — it has no Dock icon of its own and won't appear in cmd-tab. From then on it launches automatically at login; turn that off anytime in Settings ▸ General ▸ Startup.

  3. Find it in the menu bar

    Look for the Dock+ icon in your menu bar. Everything — settings, widget toggles, the edge picker — lives in that menu.

Add, remove & reorder widgets

  • Enable or disable any of the 15 widgets from Settings ▸ Widgets. Switch off what you don't use for a tighter bar.
  • Order follows your enabled list — the top of the list is the first tile on the bar. (Drag-to-reorder is on the roadmap; for now, toggle in the order you want.)
  • Pick the edge your dock lives on, and add your favorite apps for the Apps widget, all in Settings.
  • The bar caps at your screen width and scrolls when it's full — if you run all 15, disable a few or let it scroll.

Discreet by design. Collapse Dock+ to a slim handle anytime you want the screen back; one click restores it. And if you use an auto-hidden Dock, Dock+ automatically lifts up out of the way the moment the system Dock reveals, then settles back.

Permissions — and why each is needed

Dock+ only asks for what a given widget needs, and only when you first use it. macOS will prompt you; you can review and revoke any of these later in System Settings ▸ Privacy & Security. Here's the full list and the reason for each.

📅

Calendar

So the Calendar widget can show your next event and color-code it by urgency. Events are read locally and never leave your Mac.

📍

Location

So the Weather widget can fetch conditions for where you are. Only your approximate coordinates are used, and they go to Open-Meteo and nowhere else.

🤖

Automation

So Dock+ can control Music and triage Mail — play/pause, skip tracks, read your now-playing and last three messages, archive or delete inline. This uses AppleScript to talk to apps you already have open; nothing is sent off-device.

♿

Accessibility

So the Emoji widget can paste your pick into whatever app you're currently focused on. Without it, Dock+ can't insert text into other apps.

🔑

1Password CLI (Touch ID)

The 1Password widget uses 1Password's own command-line tool, op, which you authorize with Touch ID. Searches and copies happen entirely on your Mac. Copied passwords land on your clipboard and auto-clear after 90 seconds — never logged, never transmitted.

Skipping a permission only disables the widget that needs it — the rest of Dock+ keeps working. You're always in control from System Settings ▸ Privacy & Security, and there's a guided Permissions panel inside Dock+'s own Settings.

That's it

Enable the widgets you want, grant the permissions for the ones you use, and your second Dock is live. Curious about what each widget does? See the full catalog. Wondering about privacy? Read the privacy policy — Dock+ is on-device and data-light by design.
